From $600K to $3M: Building Scalable GTM Playbooks

Growth through data, experimentation, and client obsession at Xaxis

GTM Strategy Machine Learning Client Retention

The Situation

When I joined Xaxis as Account Manager in 2016, I inherited a portfolio generating ~$600,000 in annual media spend. The broader team was facing stagnation - collectively managing $3M annually with:

  • High client attrition rate (34% vs. industry average of 20-25%)
  • No differentiation in a crowded programmatic advertising market
  • Reactive account management (firefighting vs. strategic partnership)

The mandate was simple: grow revenue and retain clients. The "how" was up to me.

Strategic Approach

1. Client Acquisition: Data-Driven Targeting

  • Created custom target segments using machine learning - Xaxis had access to 1st, 2nd, and 3rd party data, enabling unique combinations of audience groups
  • Identified high-value verticals: luxury automotive, premium wellness, lifestyle brands
  • Crafted personalized pitches showing trend forecasting and media planning insights
  • Result: Secured Aston Martin, Amgen, and Alo Yoga as clients ($6M+ annual media partnership)

2. Revenue Growth: Experimentation Framework

  • Launched Xaxis's first mobile app campaign for Alo Yoga (cross-platform experimentation)
  • Leveraged ML-based custom segments to test different audience combinations and optimize performance
  • Analyzed user engagement trends across channels (web, mobile, social)
  • Optimized acquisition funnel based on cohort behavior
  • Result: 50%+ increase in user engagement; client expanded budget by 40%

3. Client Retention: Scalable GTM Playbooks

  • Documented repeatable processes for onboarding, campaign optimization, and reporting
  • Created data analytics dashboards showing ROI in real-time
  • Shifted from transactional relationships to strategic partnerships
  • Result: Client attrition decreased from 34% to <20%

Quantified Impact

  • 200%+ revenue increase for the team (from $3M to $9M annually)
  • 34% decrease in client attrition through strategic account management
  • $6M+ in new client partnerships (Aston Martin, Amgen, Alo Yoga)
  • 50%+ user engagement increase via mobile-first experimentation and ML-powered audience segmentation

Key Learnings

  1. Retention is cheaper than acquisition - But both require intentional strategy
  2. Experimentation scales differentiation - First-mover advantage creates client stickiness
  3. Data tells stories, stories build trust - Clients don't buy media plans; they buy confidence in outcomes
  4. ML-powered customization creates competitive moats - Access to data is valuable; knowing how to combine it strategically is transformational
