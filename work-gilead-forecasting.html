$40M Strategic Advantage Through ML Forecasting

Building ML-powered analytics at Gilead Sciences

Machine Learning Financial Modeling Change Management

The Situation

In 2021, Gilead's APAC/LATAM business units were struggling with forecasting accuracy. Manual processes, siloed data sources, and inconsistent methodologies meant:

  • Forecasts were off by 12-15% on average
  • Senior leadership lacked confidence in budget allocations, affecting QBRs, semi-annual financial reviews, and annual planning cycles
  • Cross-departmental projects were nearly impossible to budget when different teams used different forecasting assumptions
  • Resource allocation decisions were made on unreliable projections

I was tasked with improving forecasting reliability to support strategic decision-making across regions.

Strategic Approach

1. Root Cause Analysis

  • Conducted stakeholder interviews across 6 business units
  • Identified pain points: some teams used Excel, others used SQL, some used specific budgetary tools - no consistency
  • Teams were using different data sources for the same metrics
  • No version control or standardized assumptions

2. ML Analytics Tool Development

  • Partnered with data science team to build predictive model incorporating:
    • Historical sales data (5+ years)
    • Market intelligence signals (pricing, competitor launches, regulatory changes)
    • Logistics and supply chain variables
  • Designed intuitive interface for non-technical users
  • Built scenario planning capabilities (base case and worst case)

3. Change Management & Adoption

  • Ran pilot with 2 business units before full rollout
  • Created training materials and ran 10+ workshops
  • Established feedback loops to continuously improve model accuracy

Quantified Impact

  • Improved forecasting accuracy by 10% (from 12-15% error to 2-5% error)
  • $40M impact through better resource allocation and inventory management
  • Faster decision-making: Forecast generation time reduced from 2 weeks to 3 days
  • Gave leadership confidence in budgetary and financial planning - enabling better roadmapping and strategic prioritization for future projects

Key Learnings

  1. Technology is only 30% of the solution - The other 70% is change management and adoption
  2. Build for users, not data scientists - The best model is useless if business units won't use it
  3. Incremental accuracy compounds - 10% improvement sounds small, but at $400M+ revenue scale, it's transformational
